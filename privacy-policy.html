ArnMesh Privacy Policy

Effective date: April 27, 2026

Overview

ArnMesh is a private mesh networking app for Windows PCs and Android devices. It is designed to connect approved devices into a local-style private network for home access, trusted device sharing, and LAN-style gaming. ArnMesh is not an anonymity VPN provider and is not intended to route all internet traffic through the app.

Core privacy approach

ArnMesh tries to keep private data on your devices. Profiles, local settings, private keys, and tunnel configuration are intended to be stored locally. The coordinator and relay exist to help devices find each other, approve pairing, and fall back when direct UDP is not reachable.

Information stored on your device

ArnMesh may store profiles, device names, device identifiers, mesh IP addresses, .arnmesh hostnames, public keys, private tunnel keys, management signing keys, invite tokens, pending requests, trusted device permissions, app settings, service state, and diagnostic logs. This data is needed for the app to remember your mesh networks and reconnect after restarts or updates.

Information processed by the coordinator

When coordinator support is enabled, ArnMesh may send technical metadata required for pairing, discovery, approvals, online status, and relay fallback. This may include network/profile identifiers, device identifiers, device names, platform type, public key fingerprints, WireGuard public keys, mesh IP addresses, hostnames, invite or approval state, trusted-device permissions, endpoint candidates, public IP/port information visible to the server, last seen times, and relay availability.

The coordinator should not receive your private WireGuard keys or management signing private keys. Public keys and fingerprints may be shared because other approved devices need them to establish secure connections and verify management actions.

Relay fallback

ArnMesh prefers direct UDP connections. If direct connectivity is not available, relay fallback can forward encrypted tunnel packets between approved devices. The relay is designed to be blind to decrypted payloads, but it may still process technical metadata such as source and destination endpoints, timing, packet sizes, connection state, and traffic volume. The relay should not need private keys to operate.

Traffic routing

ArnMesh is intended to route traffic for mesh devices, such as 100.90.x.x addresses and mesh hostnames. It is not designed to become a full internet VPN by default. Your normal internet traffic should continue to use your regular network unless you change system routing or app settings outside the intended mesh workflow.

Permissions

The Windows app may require administrator permission to install its local service, tunnel driver usage, firewall rules, and uninstall entry. The Android app may use Android VPN Service permission for the mesh tunnel and may request camera access when QR code scanning is used. Notifications may be used for status or background operation when supported by the platform.

Downloads and updates

ArnMesh for Windows and Android may be distributed through GitHub Releases or other official Arn1ko Studio links. Download platforms, browsers, operating systems, hosting providers, and app stores may process technical download and security-check information according to their own policies.

Data sharing

ArnMesh does not sell personal data and is not built around advertising analytics. Technical data may be processed by services required to operate the website, downloads, coordinator, relay, VPS hosting, email support, operating systems, app stores, and GitHub. If you invite another device, that approved device may receive mesh metadata needed to connect to the profile.

Security and responsibility

ArnMesh uses encryption and approval flows to reduce risk, but no network tool can guarantee perfect protection. You are responsible for which devices you invite, which profiles you share, how you protect your devices, and whether you install files from official sources. To the maximum extent permitted by law, Arn1ko Studio is not responsible for data loss, disclosure, compromise, unauthorized access, or damage caused by user configuration, trusted or compromised devices, copied invite codes, third-party hosting, network operators, device malware, operating system behavior, unofficial or modified builds, or events outside Arn1ko Studio's control.

Deleting data

You can remove local ArnMesh data by deleting profiles, resetting the device in the app when available, clearing app data on Android, or uninstalling ArnMesh. Some coordinator or relay metadata may remain in server logs for operational, abuse-prevention, troubleshooting, or security purposes for a limited time.

Children

ArnMesh is a technical networking utility and is not designed to knowingly collect personal information from children.

Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated when ArnMesh features, coordinator behavior, relay behavior, permissions, distribution, or hosting changes. The effective date at the top of this page shows the latest version.
